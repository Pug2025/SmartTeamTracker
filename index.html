Track games live and share a spectator view for parents or staff.
  • ●Left column (red) tracks what happens to your goalie — shots, goals, and defensive breakdowns
  • ●Right column (blue) tracks your team's offense — shots for, goals for, and offensive chances
  • ✓A goal counts as a shot automatically — don't tap both
  • ⚠After each goal you'll be asked for context (screen, breakaway, etc.) — just tap what fits
  • ?Tap the ? button anytime for the full guide

How to Use Team Tracker

Getting Started

Select your team, enter the opponent name, confirm the date, and tap Start Game. Team level comes from the selected team, so you only need to set it once in Manage Teams. The period selector at the top lets you switch periods — tap Next Period between periods to advance.

Left Column — Them (Defense / Goalie)

Tap these when the opponent has the puck and creates a chance against your team.

Primary Buttons

  • Shot Against — Any shot on net. You'll be asked if it was a High Danger chance (slot area, close range). If you don't respond in 2 seconds it defaults to No. The same HD prompt appears for Shot For too.
  • Goal Against — They scored. This counts as a shot automatically. You'll be asked for context (Screen, Deflection/Tip, Cross-Crease, Net-Front Scramble, Clean Look, or Other) and game situation (EV/PP/SH).

Goalie Buttons

  • Soft Goal — A goal your goalie should have stopped. You'll be asked what went wrong: Misplay, Poor Positioning, or Other. Counts against the goalie score more heavily.
  • Bad Rebound — Goalie gave up a loose rebound. If a goal follows within 5 seconds, the goal is auto-tagged as rebound-related.

Defensive Context

  • Breakaway — Opponent got a breakaway. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it's auto-tagged. Reduces goalie blame.
  • DZ Turnover — Your team turned it over in the defensive zone. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it's auto-tagged.
  • Odd-Man Rush — Opponent has a 2-on-1, 3-on-2, etc. If a goal follows, it is auto-tagged as an odd-man-rush goal.
  • Missed Chance (No Shot) — They had a quality scoring chance but no shot on net (e.g. hit the post, went wide, fanned on it). Counts toward their expected goals at a 12% probability.
  • Penalty for Us — Your team took a penalty.

Right Column — Us (Offense)

Tap these when your team has the puck and creates chances.

Primary Buttons

  • Shot For — Your team shot on net. You'll select the shooter from your roster (or tap Unknown), then be asked if it was High Danger.
  • Goal For — Your team scored! You'll select the scorer, then optionally an assist, game situation (EV/PP/SH), and +/- players on ice.

Goalie Positive

  • Smother — Goalie covered the puck and froze play. Helps the goalie score.
  • Big Save — A save your goalie probably shouldn't have made. Gives a bonus to the goalie score. Also counts as a shot against.

Offensive Context

  • Odd Man Rush — Your team created a 2-on-1, 3-on-2, etc.
  • Breakaway — Your player got in alone.
  • Forced Turnover — Your team won the puck back through good pressure.
  • Missed Chance (No Shot) — Your team had a quality scoring chance but no shot on net (e.g. hit the post, went wide, fanned on it). Counts toward your expected goals at a 12% probability.
  • Penalty for Them — The other team took a penalty.

Automatic Tracking

  • Good Rebounds — Awarded automatically. If 4 seconds pass after a shot/big save without another shot, bad rebound, or goal, the goalie gets credit for controlling the rebound.
  • Goal Context — Breakaway and rebound goals are auto-tagged when a breakaway/bad rebound was logged within 10/5 seconds.
  • High Danger — Applies to all shots (for and against). After logging any shot, you get a 2-second prompt asking if it was HD. If you don't respond, it defaults to regular. HD shots count as ~18% goal probability vs ~7% for regular in the xG model.

Understanding the Dashboard

Chance Quality Bar

The bar under the scoreboard shows who's getting better scoring chances. Green tilting right = us. Red tilting left = them. Factors in shot danger, actual goals, and missed chances. Appears after 5+ total shots.

Goalie Score (0–100)

How well your goalie is playing relative to expected workload. Factors in: save percentage weighted by difficulty, big saves, rebound control (smothers + good rebounds vs bad rebounds), and soft goals. Context matters — goals off breakaways and screens hurt less than clean looks.

Team Score (0–100)

Overall team performance combining five areas:

  • Possession (20%) — Shot share (your shots vs total shots)
  • Danger Control (20%) — Breakaways + odd man rushes + forced turnovers created vs given up
  • Shot Quality (15%) — Are your chances better quality than theirs (xG differential)
  • Result (35%) — Goal differential, with SH goals against weighted less
  • Discipline (10%) — Penalties drawn vs penalties taken

Shot Quality Stats

  • xGF / xGA — Expected goals for/against based on shot danger. HD shots = ~18% goal probability, regular = ~7%.
  • xG Diff — Positive = you're generating better chances. Negative = they are.
  • HD Chances — High danger shots (close range, slot area).

Other Features

  • Undo — The floating UNDO button (bottom right) shows recent events. Tap any event to remove it. You'll get a brief chance to restore it.
  • Roster — Tap Roster in the header to add player numbers (one per line). These appear as quick-pick buttons when logging shots/goals for.
  • +/- Tracking — When enabled in setup, you'll be asked to select 5 players on ice after each goal (for and against) to track plus/minus.
  • Live Spectating — Tap Share Live during a game to create a view-only link for parents, staff, or another device. The spectator screen updates automatically with score, shot totals, chance quality, and momentum.
  • End Game — Tap End Game & Score to see a full game summary with goalie/team scores, stats breakdown, and exportable data.
  • Past Games — View and compare past game history from the setup screen. Swipe left on a saved game to delete it. Games are saved to the cloud when connected.
  • Season Stats — Review your active team's record, trends, and recent results from the setup screen. Reset Season clears the saved games for that team.
