How to Use Smart Team Tracker

Getting Started

Select your team (or create one), type in the opponent’s name, confirm the date, and tap Start Game.

  • Team & level — Your team’s competition level is set once inside Manage Teams and carries forward automatically.
  • Saved opponents — As you type an opponent name, previously played teams appear as suggestions. When a match is found you’ll instantly see your head-to-head record and recent scores.
  • Periods — Tap Next Period between periods to advance. You can also tap any period number at the top of the screen to jump directly to it.
  • Resume a game — If you leave mid-game, the app saves your progress. When you return, you’ll be offered the choice to Resume Game or start fresh.
  • Edit setup mid-game — Open Menu → Game Setup to change the opponent name or date without losing any tracked events.

Left Column — Them (Defense / Goalie)

Use these buttons when the opposing team has the puck and creates a chance against you.

Primary Buttons

  • Shot Against — Any opponent shot that hits the net. Shots off the post or wide do not count. After logging it, a quick prompt asks whether it was a high-danger chance (close range or from the slot area). If you don’t respond within 2 seconds it defaults to a regular-danger shot.
  • Goal Against — The opponent scored. This automatically counts as a shot, so do not log a separate Shot Against. You’ll be asked how the goal happened (Screen, Deflection/Tip, Cross-Crease Pass, Net-Front Scramble, Clean Look, or Other) and the game situation (Even Strength, Power Play, or Short Handed). If plus/minus tracking is on, you’ll also select the five skaters who were on the ice.
    Tip: If a breakaway, defensive-zone turnover, or odd-man rush led to the goal, log that event first — the goal will be linked to it automatically.
    Mis-tap? Tap Cancel Goal on the context prompt to remove it entirely.

Goalie Events

  • Soft Goal — A goal your goalie should have stopped. You’ll be asked what went wrong (Misplay, Poor Positioning, or Other) and the game situation. This counts as both a shot and a goal automatically, and it weighs more heavily against the Goalie Rating than a normal goal.
  • Bad Rebound — The goalie gave up a dangerous rebound that stayed in the scoring area. This counts as a shot against, so do not log a separate Shot Against. If a goal follows within 5 seconds, it is automatically tagged as rebound-related.

Defensive Context

  • Breakaway — An opponent got in alone on your goalie. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is automatically linked. Breakaway goals count less against the Goalie Rating because the goalie is at a disadvantage.
  • DZ Turnover (defensive-zone turnover) — Your team lost the puck in your own end. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is automatically linked.
  • Odd-Man Rush — The opponent has more attackers than defenders on a rush (2-on-1, 3-on-2, etc.). If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is automatically linked.
  • Missed Chance — The opponent had a quality scoring opportunity but no shot reached the net (post, wide, fanned on it, etc.). This still counts toward the opponent’s chance quality in the dashboard.
  • Their Penalty — The opponent committed a penalty, giving your team a power play.

Right Column — Us (Offense)

Use these buttons when your team has the puck and creates chances.

Primary Buttons

  • Shot For — Your team put a shot on net. Posts and wide shots do not count. You’ll select the shooter from your roster (or tap Unknown), then be asked whether it was a high-danger chance.
  • Goal For — Your team scored. The goal is counted the moment you tap the button, so nothing is lost if you get interrupted mid-question. You’ll then select the goal scorer, an assist (or No Assist), and the game situation; if plus/minus tracking is on, you’ll also select the five skaters on the ice. The goal automatically counts as a shot. Mis-tapped? Use the undo button.

Goalie Positives

  • Smother — Your goalie covered the puck and froze play, killing the opponent’s momentum. This gives a bonus to the Goalie Rating.
  • Big Save — An exceptional save your goalie probably should not have made (breakaway stop, cross-crease dive, etc.). This gives a significant bonus to the Goalie Rating. It automatically counts as a high-danger shot against, so do not log a separate Shot Against.

Offensive Context

  • Breakaway — Your player got in alone on the opposing goalie. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is automatically linked.
  • Forced Turnover — Your team won the puck back through pressure, a good stick check, or smart positioning. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is automatically linked.
  • Odd-Man Rush — Your team created a numbers advantage on the rush (2-on-1, 3-on-2, etc.). If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is automatically linked.
  • Missed Chance — Your team had a quality scoring opportunity but no shot reached the net (post, wide, fanned on it, etc.). This still counts toward your team’s chance quality in the dashboard.
  • Our Penalty — Your team committed a penalty, putting you short handed.

How Automatic Tracking Works

The app connects related events together so you don’t have to. Here’s what happens behind the scenes:

  • Goals count as shots — Every goal (for or against) automatically counts as a shot. Never log a separate shot after a goal.
  • Good rebounds — If 4 seconds pass after a shot or big save without another shot, rebound, or goal, your goalie gets credit for controlling the rebound.
  • Context linking — When you log a breakaway, odd-man rush, forced turnover, or defensive-zone turnover and a goal follows shortly after, the goal is automatically tagged with that context. This gives you richer post-game breakdowns without extra taps.
  • High-danger prompt — After every shot (for or against), a brief prompt asks whether it was a high-danger chance (close range, slot area). If you don’t respond within 2 seconds, it defaults to regular danger. High-danger shots carry more weight in the quality metrics.

Understanding the Live Dashboard

The dashboard updates in real time as you track. Tap any stat tile to see a brief explanation of what it measures.

Chance Quality Bar

The bar beneath the scoreboard shows which team is generating better scoring chances overall. Green tilting right means your team has the edge; red tilting left means the opponent does. It factors in shot danger, goals, and missed chances. It appears once there are at least 5 total shots in the game.

Goalie Rating (0–100)

A single number summarizing your goalie’s performance relative to expected workload. It factors in:

  • Save percentage weighted by shot difficulty (breakaway saves count more than routine stops)
  • Rebound control (smothers and clean rebounds vs. dangerous rebounds)
  • Big saves (bonus) and soft goals (penalty)
  • Context matters — goals off breakaways, screens, and deflections hurt the score less than clean-look goals

Team Rating (0–100)

An overall team performance rating that combines five areas:

  • Result (25%) — Goal differential, adjusted so that power-play goals count less and short-handed goals count more
  • Possession (20%) — Your team’s share of total shots (above 50% means you’re outshooting the opponent)
  • Danger Control (20%) — Offensive opportunities created (breakaways, odd-man rushes, forced turnovers) vs. those given up
  • Shot Quality (20%) — Whether your scoring chances are higher quality per shot than the opponent’s
  • Discipline (15%) — Penalties drawn vs. penalties taken

Dashboard Sections

Below the scores you’ll find stat tiles grouped into four sections: Goaltending (saves, big saves, smothers, rebound quality, soft goals), Shot Quality (shooting percentage, shot share, missed chances), Offensive (breakaways, odd-man rushes, forced turnovers, high-danger chances, penalties drawn), and Defensive (breakaways against, defensive-zone turnovers, odd-man rushes against, high-danger chances against, penalties taken). A By Period breakdown appears at the bottom.

A Note on Subjective Calls

Some inputs — Soft Goal, Big Save, and High Danger — are judgment calls. The scoring model trusts your observations as the coach. There is no benefit to over- or under-tagging, so just call what you see.

Strength Situations

After each goal you’ll be asked about the game situation:

  • Even Strength (EV) — Both teams have the same number of skaters (typically 5-on-5).
  • Power Play (PP) — Your team has more skaters because the opponent is serving a penalty.
  • Short Handed (SH) — Your team has fewer skaters because you are serving a penalty.

Strength data appears in the game summary breakdown and affects the Team Rating. Power-play goals are weighted less (they’re expected when you have the advantage), while short-handed goals are weighted more (they’re harder to score).

Correcting Mistakes

  • Undo button — The circular undo button floats in the corner during a game. A quick tap reverses the most recent event. A long press (hold for a moment) opens a list of the last 5 events so you can remove a specific one.
  • Event log — Tap any event in the scrolling event list to remove it. You’ll see a brief confirmation with a Restore option in case you remove the wrong one.

Roster & Player Tracking

  • Adding players — Open Menu → Roster and enter jersey numbers separated by commas (e.g. “1, 5, 12”) or one per line. These appear as quick-pick buttons when you log shots and goals for your team.
  • Auto-add — If you type a new number directly as a shooter, scorer, or assist during the game, it is automatically added to the roster for future use.
  • Plus/minus tracking — When the Track +/- toggle is turned on in setup, you’ll be asked to select the five skaters on the ice after each goal (for and against). Plus/minus tracks which players are on the ice for goals, helping you evaluate line effectiveness.
  • Player stats — Tap Player Stats on the setup screen to see each player’s cumulative goals, assists, and plus/minus across all games in the current season. Tap any player to see their game-by-game breakdown.
  • Goalies — Add goalies to a team in Manage Teams (one per line, jersey number or name). With one goalie they’re selected automatically at game start; with two or more you’ll be asked Who’s in net? and can change mid-game with the Switch button beside the goalie chip. Every save and goal against is attributed to whoever is in net, and per-goalie stats (SV%, high-danger SV%, rebound control, soft-goal rate) appear in the post-game summary and the season dashboard’s By Goalie section.

Game Summary & Exporting

Tap End Game & Score and confirm to finish tracking and see a full post-game report. The summary includes:

  • Final score with win/loss/tie result
  • Goalie Rating and Team Rating with component breakdowns (tap “How is this scored?” for details)
  • Shots and scoring stats, shot quality metrics, goaltending stats
  • Offensive and defensive context breakdowns
  • Goal-by-goal context analysis (what led to each goal for and against)
  • Strength situation breakdown (Even Strength, Power Play, Short Handed)
  • Period-by-period stats table
  • Player stats with plus/minus (when tracking is enabled)

Exporting

  • Copy to Clipboard — Creates a formatted text summary you can paste into a message, email, or team chat.
  • Export Full Data — Downloads a CSV file with every event, timestamp, and tag from the game — useful for deeper analysis in a spreadsheet.
  • Export All Data — Available in Menu → Account. Downloads all your saved games at once.

Live Spectating

During a game, tap the Share Live button in the header to create a view-only link. Anyone with the link — parents, assistant coaches, or your own second device — can follow the game in real time. The spectator view shows:

  • Team names and crests with the live score and period
  • Shot totals for both teams
  • Chance quality indicator and momentum
  • A scrolling event feed
  • A status badge — if updates pause for 30+ seconds the LIVE badge turns amber and shows when the last update arrived; when you end the game it switches to FINAL

Use Copy Link to share the URL and Stop to end the live session. Spectators cannot edit or affect your tracking — it is strictly read-only.

Season History & Opponents

  • Past Games — Tap Past Games on the setup screen to browse your game history. Filter the list with All · Wins · Losses · By Opponent (By Opponent groups games with your head-to-head record). Tap any game for a full detail view and use the arrows to step between games; delete a game from the ⋯ More menu in the detail view, or swipe left on it in the list.
  • Season Stats — Tap Season Stats to see your active team’s overall record, trends, special teams, By Goalie stats, and recent results.
  • Ending a season — In Manage Teams, each team has an End Season button that archives the current season’s games under a name like 2025–26. Archived seasons stay browsable through the season selector that appears on Past Games, Season Stats, and Player Stats. Reset Season (in the stats panels) deletes the current season’s games only — past seasons are never touched.
  • Saved opponents — Opponent names are remembered automatically. The next time you play the same team, you’ll see your head-to-head record and recent scores. To remove an opponent, clear their name from the suggestions — this also removes their associated history.
  • Manage Teams — Tap Manage Teams in setup to add, edit, or delete teams. Each team stores its own name, competition level, and roster. You can switch between multiple teams without losing any data.

Account & Cloud Sync

  • Cloud sync — When signed in, your games are automatically saved to the cloud. If you lose your device or sign in on a new one, your data comes with you.
  • Offline support — The app works fully offline. Any games tracked without a connection are queued and synced automatically the next time you’re online. The status dot in the header shows your connection state.
  • Guest mode — You can use the app without an account, but data is stored only on your current device and cannot be synced or recovered.
  • Account settings — Open Menu → Account to see your email, sync status, queued games, and plan. From here you can also export all data or delete your account.
