Welcome to Smart Team Tracker
Fast bench-side tracking with live spectating, saved opponents, and season history built in.
  • ●Red tracks chances against — shots, goals, goalie events, and defensive breakdowns.
  • ●Blue tracks your offense — shots, goals, pressure plays, and scoring chances.
  • ✓Goals count as shots automatically, and quick prompts handle strength and context for you.
  • Open Menu → Help any time for the full guide, live spectating details, and setup tips.

How to Use Smart Team Tracker

Getting Started

Select your team, enter the opponent name, confirm the date, and tap Start Game. Team level comes from the selected team, so you only set it once in Manage Teams. The opponent field lets you reuse saved opponents, add a new one from the same box, and instantly see your head-to-head record and recent scores when a match is found. The period selector at the top lets you switch periods at any time, and you can also tap Next Period between periods to advance.

Left Column — Them (Defense / Goalie)

Tap these when the opponent has the puck and creates a chance against your team.

Primary Buttons

  • Shot Against — Any shot on net. Shots off the post do not count as shots here. You'll be asked if it was a High Danger chance (slot area, close range). If you don't respond in 2 seconds it defaults to No. The same HD prompt appears for Shot For too.
  • Goal Against — They scored. This counts as a shot automatically. You'll be asked for context (Screen, Deflection/Tip, Cross-Crease, Net-Front Scramble, Clean Look, or Other) and game situation (EV/PP/SH). If +/- tracking is enabled, you'll also select the 5 skaters on ice. If a breakaway, DZ turnover, or odd-man rush triggered the goal, log that first and the goal will be auto-tagged.

Goalie Buttons

  • Soft Goal — A goal your goalie should have stopped. Counts as a shot and goal automatically. You'll be asked what went wrong (Misplay, Poor Positioning, or Other) and game situation (EV/PP/SH). Counts against the goalie score more heavily.
  • Bad Rebound — Goalie gave up a dangerous rebound. Counts as a shot against, so do not log a separate shot. If a goal follows within 5 seconds, the goal is auto-tagged as rebound-related.

Defensive Context

  • Breakaway — Opponent got a breakaway. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it's auto-tagged. Reduces goalie blame.
  • DZ Turnover — Your team turned it over in the defensive zone. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it's auto-tagged.
  • Odd-Man Rush — Opponent has a 2-on-1, 3-on-2, etc. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is auto-tagged as an odd-man-rush goal.
  • Missed Chance (No Shot) — They had a quality scoring chance but no shot on net (e.g. hit the post, went wide, fanned on it). It still counts toward their chance quality.
  • Their Penalty — The other team took a penalty (power play for us). It is counted and logged in the event list.

Right Column — Us (Offense)

Tap these when your team has the puck and creates chances.

Primary Buttons

  • Shot For — Your team shot on net. Shots off the post do not count as shots here. You'll select the shooter from your roster (or tap Unknown), then be asked if it was High Danger.
  • Goal For — Your team scored. You'll select the scorer, then optionally an assist, and game situation (EV/PP/SH). If +/- tracking is enabled, you'll also select the 5 skaters on ice.

Goalie Positive

  • Smother — Goalie covered the puck and froze play. Helps the goalie score.
  • Big Save — A save your goalie probably shouldn't have made. Gives a bonus to the goalie score. Counts as a high-danger shot against automatically (no prompts).

Offensive Context

  • Breakaway — Your player got in alone. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is auto-tagged.
  • Forced Turnover — Your team won the puck back through good pressure. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is auto-tagged.
  • Odd-Man Rush — Your team created a 2-on-1, 3-on-2, etc. If a goal follows within 10 seconds, it is auto-tagged.
  • Missed Chance (No Shot) — Your team had a quality scoring chance but no shot on net (e.g. hit the post, went wide, fanned on it). It still counts toward your chance quality.
  • Our Penalty — Your team took a penalty (shorthanded). It is counted and logged in the event list.

Automatic Tracking

  • Goals Count as Shots — Do not log a separate shot after any goal for or against.
  • Good Rebounds — Awarded automatically. If 4 seconds pass after a shot/big save without another shot, bad rebound, or goal, the goalie gets credit for controlling the rebound.
  • Goal Context — Breakaway, odd-man-rush, forced-turnover, DZ-turnover, and rebound goals are auto-tagged when the lead-in event was logged just before the goal.
  • High Danger — Applies to all shots (for and against). After logging any shot, you get a 2-second prompt asking if it was HD. If you don't respond, it defaults to regular. High-danger chances are weighted more heavily than regular ones in the live quality readouts.

Understanding the Dashboard

Chance Quality Bar

The bar under the scoreboard shows who's getting better scoring chances. Green tilting right = us. Red tilting left = them. Factors in shot danger, actual goals, and missed chances. Appears after 5+ total shots.

Goalie Score (0–100)

How well your goalie is playing relative to expected workload. Factors in: save percentage weighted by difficulty, big saves, rebound control (smothers + good rebounds vs bad rebounds), and soft goals. Context matters — goals off breakaways and screens hurt less than clean looks.

Team Score (0–100)

Overall team performance combining five areas:

  • Result (25%) — Weighted goal differential (PP goals discounted, SH goals bonused)
  • Possession (20%) — Shot share (your shots vs total shots)
  • Danger Control (20%) — Breakaways + odd man rushes + forced turnovers created vs given up
  • Shot Quality (20%) — Are your chances higher quality per shot than theirs
  • Discipline (15%) — Penalties drawn vs penalties taken

A Note on Subjective Tags

Some inputs — like Soft Goal, Big Save, and High Danger — are your judgment calls as coach. The scoring model trusts your observations. There’s no benefit to over- or under-tagging, so just call what you see.

Other Features

  • Event Removal + Undo — Tap any event in the live event list to remove it. A quick tap on the floating UNDO button reverses the last event, and a long press opens the last 5 events so you can remove a specific one.
  • Roster — Open Menu → Roster to add player numbers (one per line). These appear as quick-pick buttons when logging shots/goals for, and a player number is auto-added to the roster if you type it in as a shooter, scorer, or assister.
  • +/- Tracking — When enabled in setup, you'll be asked to select 5 players on ice after each goal (for and against) to track plus/minus.
  • Live Spectating — Tap Share Live during a game to create a view-only link for parents, staff, or another device. The spectator screen updates automatically with score, shot totals, chance quality, and momentum.
  • End Game — Tap End Game & Score to see a full game summary with goalie/team scores, stats breakdown, and exportable data.
  • Saved Opponents — The setup opponent box remembers teams you've played, shows last played dates, and can remove an opponent entirely. Deleting one also removes their saved head-to-head history and season stats.
  • Past Games — View and compare past game history from the setup screen. Swipe left on a saved game to delete it. Games are saved to the cloud when connected.
  • Season Stats — Review your active team's record, trends, and recent results from the setup screen. Reset Season clears the saved games for that team.
